# Tether Visual System

## Thesis

Tether is **philosophically streamlined and aesthetically maximal**.

The architecture remains legible: notice a person, enter the same musical moment, communicate presence, and preserve the trace. The visual system should not flatten that emotional event into conventional utility cards.

- Remove conceptual clutter, not aesthetic intensity.
- Reduce effort, not atmosphere.
- Simplify navigation, not desire.
- Let human proximity determine visual intensity.

## Semantic color

- **Violet:** relationship, possibility, and Tether identity.
- **Teal:** synchronization, availability, and successful presence.
- **Amber:** Knock, waiting, and asynchronous capsules.
- **Rose:** dating signal, attraction, and deliberate romantic intent.
- **Red:** safety and destructive action only.

## Motion hierarchy

1. **Ambient:** one slow liquid field that makes the product feel alive without demanding attention.
2. **Presence:** portraits, rings, and signal light move only when a real person or session is live.
3. **Intent:** consequential controls respond clearly; routine controls remain quiet.
4. **Ritual:** joining, Pulse, session endings, and Memory Anchors receive the strongest transformation.

## Surface hierarchy

Quiet surfaces: settings, search, permissions, conversations, errors, and provider configuration.

Expressive destinations: Now Playing, Profiles, Wavelength, Dating, Shared Stage, Pulse, Memory Anchors, and Time Capsules.

## Profiles

Profiles are aesthetic territories, not resumes. They communicate presence, musical interior, evidence of connection, immediate actions, and shared history in that order. Public reach metrics and ranking dashboards do not belong in the primary experience.

## Dating

Dating is an explicit secondary world. It may be lush, romantic, and visually dramatic because the user intentionally enters it. Identity, orientation, intent, and optional physical descriptors never appear in ordinary Tether onboarding and are exposed only after explicit opt-in.

A dating gesture should be musical: leave a song, enter a listening moment, or open a profile. Tether should not reduce attraction to a generic like button.

## Product rule

The system is quiet when no meaningful human signal is present. Visual intensity increases with relational proximity: seeing someone live, opening their interior, entering the same second, receiving a Pulse, and preserving the resulting memory.
