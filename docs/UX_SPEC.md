# Tether UX specification

## Listen

The first screen answers three questions immediately: what am I hearing, who is available, and what relationship should I continue? The primary action uses current playback and saved privacy. Exploration stays collapsed.

## People

Close relationships are the default. Availability is second. Explore is explicit. Each person is described with evidence—shared artists, listening habits, mutual context, and broad availability—rather than a magic percentage. Messages remain attached to people and moments.

## You

Identity stays musical and compact. Privacy is editable in human language. Memory Anchors and Time Capsules preserve relationships without turning the profile into a public performance dashboard.

## Stage

The Stage prioritizes person, track, synchronization state, Pulse, invitation, and queue. Normal Pulse is immediate; amplified Pulse is a deliberate hold. Ending a meaningful session creates an Anchor automatically.

## Motion

One ambient layer establishes atmosphere. Live presence may breathe. Consequential controls respond. Joining, receiving Pulse, and completing a session may celebrate. Errors use one brief corrective motion. Reduced-motion settings disable nonessential animation.

## Typography and density

Metadata should ordinarily remain at or above 13px. Monospace belongs only to genuine timing or synchronization telemetry. Track title and human identity outrank decorative labels.
